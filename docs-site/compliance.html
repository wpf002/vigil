Compliance

VIGIL ships exportable evidence packs for SOC 2 Type II, PCI-DSS, and NIST CSF. Generate them from Compliance in the web app or call the reporting endpoints directly.

SOC 2 Type II evidence pack

GET /compliance/soc2?period_days=30
Authorization: Bearer <jwt|api_key>

Returns three criteria:

  • CC6 — Logical Access. Per-tenant user accounts, roles, last login, active flag.
  • CC7 — System Operations. AttackState counts by status, detection coverage score, response-time distribution.
  • CC8 — Change Management. Detection version history with deploy timestamps, rollbacks.

PCI-DSS evidence pack

  • Req 10 — Audit Logs. AttackState transition counts and evidence-chain completeness.
  • Req 11 — Security Testing. Detection coverage score and last detection deployment.

NIST CSF mapping

  • Identify. Coverage by ATT&CK tactic.
  • Protect. Active detection count.
  • Detect. Total detection fires, average false-positive rate.
  • Respond. Mean time to resolve, playbook completion rate.
  • Recover. Attacks resolved, mean recovery time.

Audit log

VIGIL records an append-only audit_log row for every:

  • user.login, user.logout, user.created
  • api_key.created, api_key.revoked
  • webhook.registered
  • attack.status_changed, detection.rollback, playbook.started

The table has no DELETE path exposed via any endpoint — retention is unlimited by design. Read via GET /auth/audit-log or GET /compliance/audit-log.

Initiating a SOC 2 Type II audit

VIGIL's exports cover the system-monitoring portion of a SOC 2 audit. They are not a substitute for the audit itself.

  1. Engage a licensed CPA firm. Typical timeline: 6–12 months from engagement to attestation.
  2. Auditors will request:
    • An auditable evidence-collection window of at least 6 months.
    • Documented policies (access control, change management, incident response).
    • Continuous monitoring evidence — VIGIL's exports satisfy the SIEM portion.
    • Pen-test results from a separate firm.
  3. Pair the SOC 2 export with HR onboarding records, vendor management, and your risk register.
