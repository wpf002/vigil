Competitive Position

VIGIL replaces alert queues with attack-state narratives. Here's how it stacks up against incumbents.

Feature comparison

When VIGIL wins

  • Mid-market security teams who want their own detections but lack staff to run a custom correlation engine.
  • Multi-SIEM orgs — write a detection once, deploy to Splunk and Sentinel.
  • Compliance-driven buyers who need SOC 2 evidence without buying a separate GRC tool.
  • Teams replacing GreyMatter who can't tolerate vendor-owned detection logic.

When VIGIL is not the answer

  • Pure log-storage replacement — VIGIL is not a SIEM, it sits on top of one.
  • Sub-100-event/sec environments where a managed MDR makes more economic sense.
